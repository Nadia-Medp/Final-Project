Introduction to Impressionism

Les Glacons - Claude Monet

Discover the Beauty of Light and Color

The Impressionist movement, which emerged in France in the late 19th century, revolutionized the art world with its focus on capturing the fleeting effects of light and color in everyday life. Unlike traditional art that emphasized detail and realism, Impressionists aimed to convey the atmosphere of a scene, often painting en plein air (outdoors) to directly observe natural light. They used loose, rapid brushstrokes and bright, unmixed colors to depict moments in time, such as bustling cityscapes, landscapes, and leisure activities. The movement emphasized personal perception over precise representation, making it a pioneering step toward modern art. Key figures include Claude Monet, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, and Edgar Degas.
